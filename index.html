01

Overview & method

Swiss large caps remain a core destination for hard-currency income without giving up the prospect of capital appreciation. This screen takes the twenty SMI constituents and surfaces twelve names that pair an above-market dividend yield with positive 12-month analyst total-return potential.

01

Income anchors. Swiss Re, Partners Group and Zurich offer the richest yields (~5–6%), backed by capital-return-focused franchises.

02

Balanced compounders. Roche, Novartis and Nestlé combine ~3.3–4% yields with double-digit consensus upside and defensive earnings.

03

Growth tilt. UBS and Holcim trade lower yields for the highest analyst-implied price appreciation in the group.

04

Watch the coupon-only names. Swisscom yields ~3.6% but screens as a consensus Sell on valuation — income today, capital-loss risk on the target.

Method. The universe is the SMI. Names were screened qualitatively for (i) an indicative dividend yield at or above the broad Swiss-market average and (ii) the 12-month consensus price-target signal, then grouped by return tilt. Yields and analyst targets are indicative figures gathered from public mid-2026 sources, are rounded, and move continuously. This note is for information only and is not investment advice.

03

Income leaders

Yield-led, capital-return franchises

Swiss Re (SREN) is the highest-yielding name in the screen — a forward yield near 5.9% from a diversified global reinsurer with strong solvency and a progressive ordinary-dividend policy. The total-return case is primarily the coupon; consensus upside to target is only mid-single-digit, and catastrophe experience is the key swing factor.

Partners Group (PGHN) pairs a ~5.6% yield with the largest analyst-implied upside in the group (mean target ~CHF 1,156 versus a depressed share price). It is both an income and a recovery story, but the upside dispersion is very wide and earnings are sensitive to private-markets fundraising and realisations.

Zurich Insurance (ZURN) offers a secure, well-covered ~5% dividend from a high-quality composite insurer. We frame it as an income anchor: upside estimates diverge sharply across vendors, so the total return is dominated by the distribution rather than multiple expansion.

Swisscom (SCMN) yields ~3.6% with classic telecom defensiveness, but it screens as a consensus Sell with the average target roughly 17% below the current price. It belongs here for income, with the explicit caveat that analysts see valuation downside — a coupon-only holding.

04

Balanced total-return

Yield plus credible capital gain

Roche (ROG) and Novartis (NOVN) are the sweet spot of the screen: ~3.5%+ yields, defensive pharmaceutical earnings, multi-decade dividend-growth records and ~+11% consensus upside on constructive (Buy-biased) ratings. Pipeline delivery is the swing factor for both.

Nestlé (NESN) is the low-volatility staples compounder — a ~3.3% yield, one of Europe's longest dividend-growth streaks, and ~+10% upside as a below-trend multiple recovers on a return to volume growth.

SGS (SGSN) and Kühne+Nagel (KNIN) round out the balanced bucket with ~3.5% yields. SGS offers steady testing-and-inspection cash flows and a Buy-biased consensus; Kühne+Nagel adds a more cyclical, logistics-driven recovery angle and was recently upgraded to Buy.

05

Growth tilt & risks

Lower yield, higher upside

UBS (UBSG) carries the highest analyst upside in the screen (~+18%) on a ~2.5% yield — a Credit Suisse-integration and capital-normalisation story where income is a supplement, not the core. Holcim (HOLN) offers a cyclical re-rating on ~2.5–3% yield and ~+8% upside, while ABB (ABBN) is the lowest yielder (~1.4%) but a structural electrification grower with a recently raised target.

For income first, lead with Swiss Re, Partners Group and Zurich. For a balance of yield and capital gain — the screen's objective — Roche, Novartis and Nestlé stand out. For a growth tilt, UBS and Holcim offer the most upside at the cost of current income.

Risks & caveats

This is a screen, not a recommendation, and not investment advice. Analyst price targets are weak predictors of realised return and dispersed widely for some names (notably Zurich and Partners Group). Dividends are not guaranteed and can be cut. As a Swiss resident, factor in the 35% withholding tax (reclaimable for residents) and how the income fits your overall tax position. Yields and targets shift continuously — verify live figures before acting.

Hard-currency income, with selective upside — but the coupon does the heavy lifting.
